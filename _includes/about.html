About the Project

Part AR gallery. Part guided tour. All Bartow.

Bartow Unframed started with a simple idea: public murals are already doing civic work — marking places, telling stories, drawing people into the city. What would happen if those murals could move, speak, and respond? What if they could point you, while you stood in front of them, to the coffee shop two blocks away?

The app stitches four AR mural experiences into a single guided tour of downtown Bartow. Each mural has its own animation, its own audio, its own small world that opens when you raise your camera. Between the murals, an OpenStreetMap-powered map surfaces local businesses, points of interest, and routes — the art and the city, threaded through the same interface.

It was made through community engagement: stakeholder meetings, public presentations, direct collaboration with the Bartow CRA and local business owners. The finished app is designed to run on low-end phones, not just flagship devices — because accessibility to public art should include accessibility to the technology that animates it.
